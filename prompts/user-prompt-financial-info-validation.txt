FINANCIAL INFORMATION VALIDATION REVIEW

PURPOSE
- Review an assembled draft of Section 12, "Financial Information".
- Validate the draft against the supplied accountant report and retrieved evidence.
- Check alignment with the target prospectus structure and tone.
- Do NOT rewrite the draft and do NOT generate replacement disclosure.

VALIDATION OBJECTIVE
- Identify unsupported claims, altered figures, section misclassification, duplication, terminology drift, missing required tables where the evidence is tabular, and overuse of fallback wording.

SECTIONS IN SCOPE
- 12.1 Historical Financial Information
- 12.1.1 Consolidated statements of profit or loss and other comprehensive income
- 12.1.2 Consolidated statements of financial position
- 12.1.3 Consolidated statements of cash flows
- 12.2 Capitalisation and Indebtedness
- 12.3 Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations
- 12.4 Liquidity and Capital Resources

VALIDATION CHECKS
1) Numerical consistency
- Confirm all figures, dates, periods, units, labels, notes, and placeholders match the evidence.
- Flag any inferred, reconstructed, calculated, rounded, or reformatted figures that are not supported by the evidence.

2) Evidence support
- Confirm every factual statement is supported by the evidence.
- Flag speculation, unsupported causal explanations, invented accounting references, and unsupported cross-references.

3) Section scope compliance
- Confirm each section contains only content appropriate to that heading.
- Flag content that belongs in another section, including duplicated ratio, borrowing, litigation, or dividend content.

4) Structure and table compliance
- Confirm the draft follows the target section structure.
- Flag missing tables where the evidence is tabular and the section is expected to reproduce the table.
- Flag cases where prose replaces a material source table without preserving the disclosed content.

5) Tone and disclosure compliance
- Confirm neutral prospectus tone.
- Flag promotional, speculative, reassuring, or forward-looking language that is not expressly disclosed in the evidence.

6) Internal consistency
- Check consistency of terminology, defined periods, accounting framework references, and section cross-references across the draft.

7) Fallback discipline
- Flag any use of "Not disclosed in the provided documents." where the section is partially or fully supported by the evidence.
- Flag sections that appear unsupported but were drafted as if supported.

OUTPUT FORMAT
Provide findings under the following headings only:
- Numerical Consistency Issues
- Evidence Support Issues
- Duplication or Misclassification Issues
- Structure or Table Compliance Issues
- Tone / Disclosure Compliance Issues
- Internal Consistency Issues
- General Observations

If no material issues are identified, state exactly:
"No material inconsistencies identified based on the provided evidence."

RESTRICTIONS
- Do NOT rewrite any section.
- Do NOT propose replacement wording.
- Do NOT add new disclosures.
- Maintain neutral professional tone.
- Do NOT mention AI, prompts, or internal drafting process.
