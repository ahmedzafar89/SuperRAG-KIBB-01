# Step 1
---

Based strictly on the provided evidence documents, identify IPO risk factor themes.

Generate ONLY a structured outline under:

9.1 RISKS RELATING TO OUR BUSINESS AND OUR OPERATIONS
9.2 RISKS RELATING TO THE INDUSTRY IN WHICH WE OPERATE
9.3 RISKS RELATING TO INVESTMENT IN OUR SHARES
9.4 OTHER RISKS

EXPECTED COVERAGE:

9.1 Business & Operations
- Aim for approximately 10–15 distinct risks where supported by evidence.
- Prioritise operational, financial, compliance, technology, customer concentration, personnel, governance, execution, dependency, and business continuity risks.
- Prefer document-specific risks over generic risks.
- If evidence is insufficient, generate fewer risks rather than introducing speculative or boilerplate risks.

9.2 Industry Risks
- Generate only 2–5 risks.
- Focus strictly on external industry-wide factors affecting multiple companies (e.g., macroeconomic conditions, competition, regulatory landscape, market demand, sector disruption).
- Do NOT include company-specific operational, governance, or financial risks.
- Avoid repetition of risks already included in 9.1.

9.3 Shares & Listing Risks
- Generate only 2–3 investor-related risks.
- Focus strictly on capital market exposure such as: listing uncertainty, liquidity, dividends, trading volatility, and absence of prior public market where applicable.
- Exclude operational, industry, regulatory business, or governance risks.
- Avoid duplication with other sections.

9.4 Other Risks
- Optional section.
- Include only material risks that clearly do not fit within Business & Operations, Industry, or Shares & Listing.
- Maximum 0–2 risks.
- If no such risks exist, omit this section entirely.

STRICT CLASSIFICATION RULES:
- Business operations, revenue, personnel, systems, compliance, governance, financial dependencies → 9.1
- Broad industry trends affecting multiple companies → 9.2
- Share price, dividends, listing process, investor exposure → 9.3
- Residual or exceptional risks not otherwise classified → 9.4

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
- Each risk heading MUST begin with: “We”, “Our Group”, or “Our business”.
- Provide headings only — no explanations, elaborations, or descriptive paragraphs.
- Avoid duplication or overlap across sections.
- Prefer document-supported risks at all times.
- Do not invent risks beyond available evidence.
- If evidence is insufficient for a section, state exactly: “Not disclosed in the provided documents.”

FINAL CONSISTENCY CHECK (MANDATORY BEFORE OUTPUT):
- Ensure each risk appears in only one section.
- Reassign any misclassified risks.
- Remove duplicated or overlapping risks.
- Confirm section counts broadly align with IPO prospectus conventions.
